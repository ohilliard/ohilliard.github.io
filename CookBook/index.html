Japenese Creme Sanwiches

Ingredients

  • 5 Japanese Milk bread alices
  • Vegetable oil
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3/4 cup whole milk
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoon constarch
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • Strawberries
  • Granulated sugar

Directions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract.
  2. Whisk the mixture until stiff peaks form
  3. Cover the whipped cream and store it in the refrigerator until ready to use
  4. Heat a shallow layer of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  5. Fry each slice of Japanese milk bread until golden and crispy on both sides, flipping carefully to prevent tearing.
  6. Transfer the fried bread to a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  7. Cut each fried bread slice in half and gently open each piece to create a pocket.
  8. Fill the pocket generously with whipped cream and top with three strawberry halves.
  9. Cut each fried bread slice in half and gently open each piece to create a pocket.
  10. Fill the pocket about two-thirds of the way with whipped cream, leaving room for the custard.
  11. Spoon custard into the remaining one-third of the pocket.
  12. Sprinkle an even layer of granulated sugar over the custard and brûlée it with a kitchen torch until caramelized and golden. Allow the caramelized sugar to cool and harden slightly before serving.

Honey Butter Toast

Ingregiants

  • 6 slices of white sandwich bread
  • 1 cup unsalted melted butter
  • 1/3 cup honey
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  2. If you're using thin white sandwich bread, use 12 slices and stack two on top of each other to make 6 thicker pieces. Just make sure the bread is not too soft—something like fresh Wonder Bread will not hold its shape well. If you're using a whole loaf,slice it into pieces about 1.75 to 2 inches thick.
  3. Slice off all the crusts before moving on
  4. In a microwave-safe bowl, mix together the melted unsalted butter, honey, light brown sugar, vanilla, and a pinch of salt. Microwave for 30 seconds to 1 minute, then whisk really well until it turns into a smooth, glossy syrup.
  5. Coat each piece of bread generously on all sides with the syrup and place them on a nonstick baking pan—no parchment needed.
  6. Bake for 15 minutes, then carefully flip each one and bake for another 5 minutes until golden and sticky all over.
  7. Let them cool for about 10 minutes before serving. They will feel soft right out of the oven but will crisp up beautifully as they cool slightly. Enjoy them warm!
