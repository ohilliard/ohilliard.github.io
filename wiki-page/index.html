Crown shyness

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Crown shyness (also canopv disengagement I canopy shvness [2] or inter-crown spacing [3]) is a phenomenon observed in some tree species, in which the crowns of fully stocked trees do not touch each other, forming a canopy with channel-like gaps[4][5] The phenomenon is most prevalent among trees of the same species, but also occurs between trees of different species[6][7] There exist many hypotheses as to why crown shyness is an adaptive behavior, and research suggests that it might inhibit spread of leaf-eating insect larvae[8]
